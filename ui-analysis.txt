Account Information Form - UI Analysis


a. Problems found

Security:
- The card number field is not masked ,anyone nearby can see it.
- There is no CVV field. 
- The payment amount (30.00) is hardcoded on the client side.

Usability:
- The "MI" field has no explanation. Non-US users won't know what it means.
- There are two address fields but the second one has no label. It's not clear what it's for.
- No currency symbol or indication next to the payment amount. 
- "State or Province" is a dropdown, which seems to be US/Canada focused. If this form is international, many countries don't have states at all (e.g. Israel). Users from those countries have no valid option to select.
- The expiration date uses two separate dropdowns (month and year). It seems possible to select a month without selecting a year and vice versa, which should not be allowed.
- It is unclear whether expired dates are blocked in the dropdowns (needs testing).


b. Test cases

Test 1 - Happy path:
Fill all fields with valid data and click Continue.
Expected: form submits and moves to the next step.

Test 2 - Missing required field:
Leave the Card Number field empty, fill everything else, click Continue.
Expected: form does not submit and shows an error on the empty field.

Test 3 - Invalid card number:
Enter an invalid value like "abcd" or "1234" in the Card Number field, click Continue.
Expected: validation error is shown and form does not submit.

Test 4 - Partial expiration date (month only):
Select a month but leave the year as "Select year", click Continue.
Expected: validation error, form does not submit.

Test 5 - Expired card date:
Select a month and year that are already in the past, click Continue.
Expected: validation error saying the card is expired, form does not submit.


c. Most severe bug and suggested fix

The missing CVV field is the most severe issue. CVV exists specifically to verify that the person submitting the form physically has the card. 
Without it, anyone with a stolen card number can complete the form.
Fix: 
Add a CVV input next to the expiration date. It should be masked (like a password field), 
limited to 3-4 digits depending on the card type (Visa/Mastercard/Discover use 3 digits, Amex uses 4). 
The CVV field length should update automatically based on the selected card type.
