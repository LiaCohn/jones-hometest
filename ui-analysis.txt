Account Information Form - UI Analysis


a. Problems found

Security:
- No CVV field. Without it, anyone with a stolen card number can submit the form.

Usability:
- "MI" has no explanation. Most non-US users won't know what it means.
- Two address fields but only the first one is labeled.
- Payment amount shows 30.00 with no currency symbol.
- "State or Province" dropdown won't work for countries that don't have states (e.g. Israel).
- Expiration date is two separate dropdowns - you can select a month without a year.
- Not sure if past expiration dates are blocked - needs testing.


b. Test cases

Test 1 - Happy path:
Fill all fields with valid data, click Continue.
Expected: form submits and moves to the next step.

Test 2 - Missing required field:
Leave Card Number empty, click Continue.
Expected: error shown on the empty field, form doesn't submit.

Test 3 - Invalid card number:
Enter "abcd" or "1234" in Card Number, click Continue.
Expected: validation error, form doesn't submit.

Test 4 - Partial expiration date:
Select a month, leave Year as "Select year", click Continue.
Expected: validation error, form doesn't submit.

Test 5 - Expired card:
Select a past month and year, click Continue.
Expected: error saying the card is expired, form doesn't submit.


c. Most severe bug and fix

Missing CVV field. Without it the form is easy to abuse with stolen card numbers.

Fix: add a CVV input next to the expiration date. 3 digits for Visa/Mastercard/Discover, 
     4 for Amex - should update automatically based on the selected card type.
